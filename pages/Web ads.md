- ## Sizes
	- "Big Four" of the advertising world. If you look at major news sites or digital magazines, almost every ad slot you see is built to one of those specific pixel dimensions.
	- ### Why these sizes matter
		- **Design Consistency:** Using these ensures your banners don't look stretched or cut off.
		- **Third-Party Compatibility:** If you ever decide to run ads from Google AdSense or another network, these are the only sizes they will provide.
		- **Asset Reuse:** Your design team only needs to make 3 or 4 versions of an ad, and they will fit on 99% of websites.
	- ### The sizes
		- | **Name** | **Dimensions (px)** | **Aspect Ratio (Simplified)** | **Visual Description** |
		  | --- | --- | --- | --- |
		  | **Wide Skyscraper** | **160 x 600** | **~1:4** | Very tall and thin. |
		  | **Medium Rectangle** | **300 x 250** | **6:5** | Almost a square, but slightly wider. |
		  | **Leaderboard** | **728 x 90** | **~8:1** | A very long, thin horizontal strip. |
		  | **Mobile Leaderboard** | **320 x 50** | **~6:1** | A short, wide strip for phones. |
